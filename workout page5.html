 Benefits of Exercise

Bodyweight squats

Bodyweight squats can increase lower body and core strength as they work the abs, buttocks, hips, thighs, calves, and shins.

How to do them:

  1. Stand with the feet slightly wider than hip-width apart, angling the toes slightly outward.

  2. Keep the hands down by the sides, with the palms facing in and keep the shoulders back.

  3. Engage the abdominal muscles to support the back.

  4. Shift the hips back and bend the knees as though taking a seat, keeping a flat back.

  5. Keep lowering down to the ground until the thighs are parallel with the floor.

  6. Push through the feet to straighten back up into the starting position.

  7. Inhale into the squat, then exhale when standing back up.

Planks

Planks strengthen the back and abdominal muscles and help build core strength.

How to do them:

  1. Start with the elbows and lower arms on the floor, keeping the elbows in line with the shoulders.

  2. Lift the body so that it forms a straight line horizontal to the floor.

  3. Keep the feet together and the toes flexed to support the body.

  4. Hold for 20–30 seconds.

  5. Slowly lower to the floor and rest for 1 minute, then repeat 3–5 times.
